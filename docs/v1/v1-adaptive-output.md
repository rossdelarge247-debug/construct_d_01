# V1 Adaptive Output Model

## Principle

V1 output is not binary. It scales based on what the user was able to provide. The system never pretends to have more than it does, and never shames the user for having less.

## The constant: process clarity

Every user — regardless of how much they provide — leaves V1 understanding what the separation/divorce process looks like for their specific situation.

This is the guaranteed deliverable. It is generated from the situation snapshot (Step 2) and is always personalised. It is free, unconditional, and the primary hook.

## The variable: plan depth

### Readiness assessment

The system calculates a readiness score from:

- **Chapters completed** vs. skipped (situation, route, children, home, finances, confidence mapping)
- **Confidence distribution** — ratio of Known/Estimated to Unsure/Unknown across domains
- **Core domain coverage** — whether children (if relevant), housing (if relevant), and finances have any substantive input

This is not a precise score — it's a tier selection.

### Four output tiers

#### Tier 1 — Full plan

**Triggers:** Most chapters completed. Substantive input across relevant domains. Confidence map has a meaningful mix of Known/Estimated items.

**Plan output:**
- Rich provisional plan across all areas (children, housing, finances)
- Strength assessment per area ("Your children arrangements are well-shaped. Your housing aims are clear but depend on the financial picture.")
- Confidence map with visual differentiation
- Specific feedback on what looks strong and what's tentative
- Targeted recommendations

**Next steps:**
- Primary: "Build your financial picture" (V2 entry)
- Secondary: specific actions to resolve highest-priority unknowns
- Clear V2+ bridge with concrete benefits

**Conversion messaging:**
- "You've built a strong starting position. Save your workspace to keep your plan and continue."

---

#### Tier 2 — Partial plan

**Triggers:** Some chapters completed, others skipped or very light. Confidence map has significant Unsure/Unknown items but some substance exists.

**Plan output:**
- Covers areas with substance, explicitly calls out gaps
- "Your children arrangements look considered. Your financial aims are captured but broad. Housing is something you haven't explored yet — and that's fine."
- Confidence map shows the mix honestly
- Recommendations focus on strengthening the weaker areas

**Next steps:**
- Mix of "complete your plan" actions and V2 entry points
- "Return to housing to strengthen your plan"
- "Build your financial picture to fill in the gaps"

**Conversion messaging:**
- "You've made real progress. Save your workspace to keep building."

---

#### Tier 3 — Thin plan

**Triggers:** Minimal input. Most domains are Unsure or Unknown. User completed situation and route but skipped or lightly touched the planning sections.

**Plan output:**
- Honest acknowledgement: "You're early in this — that's fine."
- Shows what was captured, however minimal
- Frames it as a starting point, not a failure
- Route clarity is still prominent (this was fully generated)

**Next steps:**
- Focus on returning to strengthen the plan: "When you're ready, tell us about your children / your home / your finances"
- Softer V2 signposting: "The more you can tell us, the more we can help"
- No commercial pressure

**Conversion messaging:**
- "Save your progress so you can pick up where you left off."

---

#### Tier 4 — Not ready yet

**Triggers:** Below minimum threshold. User completed situation snapshot and saw their route, but provided almost nothing for the planning sections. Mostly skipped or all Unknown.

**Plan output:**
- No pretend plan generated
- Process clarity reinforced: "Understanding the process is a huge first step."
- Clear framing: "When you're ready to start shaping your plan, everything is here waiting for you."
- If safeguarding flags present: support resources prominently shown

**Next steps:**
- Gentle re-entry points: "When you're ready: come back and start with whichever area feels most manageable"
- No V2 upsell
- Support resources if relevant

**Conversion messaging:**
- "Save your session so everything is here when you're ready."

---

## How this affects the workspace

All tiers generate a workspace. The difference is what's in it:

| Tier | Workspace contents |
|------|-------------------|
| Full plan | Route card + plan summary card + cards across all sections + next steps card |
| Partial plan | Route card + plan summary card + cards for completed sections + gap indicators + next steps card |
| Thin plan | Route card + minimal cards + "build your plan" prompts + next steps card |
| Not ready | Route card + next steps card focused on re-entry |

The workspace is never empty. At minimum it has the route card and next steps. But the density and richness scales with input.

## How this affects the PDF

All tiers except "not ready" generate a downloadable PDF:

- Full plan: comprehensive report
- Partial plan: report with explicit gap sections
- Thin plan: brief summary with "areas to explore" sections
- Not ready: no PDF offered (nothing substantive to download)

## How this affects commercial conversion

| Tier | Commercial posture |
|------|-------------------|
| Full plan | Strong V2 upsell: "Make this plan concrete with your financial picture" |
| Partial plan | Moderate: "Strengthen your plan and build the financial detail" |
| Thin plan | Soft: "The more you can tell us, the more we can help" |
| Not ready | No commercial pressure. Pure value delivery. "We're here when you need us." |

The product earns the right to ask for money by delivering value first. If it hasn't delivered much value yet, it doesn't ask.
