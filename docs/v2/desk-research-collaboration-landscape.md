# Professional Landscape: Tools, Workflows & Gaps

**Sources:** Solicitor blogs, Resolution guidance, judicial commentary, product research, Law Commission report.

## How solicitors currently work

1. Client gathers documents over 2-4 weeks (chased by solicitor at £200-500/hr)
2. Solicitor drafts Form E in Word/PDF, attaching exhibits
3. Served on other solicitor via email or post
4. Questionnaire exchanged — formal follow-up queries (Word document)
5. Answers exchanged (often multiple rounds)
6. Both sets feed into court hearing bundle

**Cost driver:** Chasing missing documents. Hay & Kilner reported 30 minutes to 4 hours per matter just organising bank data before Armalytix.

**No standardised format.** Disclosure arrives as PDFs, photos, Word files, spreadsheets, physical paper. No common data interchange between firms.

**Email is the collaboration layer.** Proposals exchanged as Word docs with tracked changes, or as separate letters. No shared workspace.

## How mediators currently work

1. Custom forms (simpler than Form E) — each mediation service has their own
2. Physical document exchange in sessions (literally photocopying)
3. Mediator displays figures on screen during sessions — the closest thing to a "shared workspace"
4. Two output documents: Open Financial Statement (OFS) + Memorandum of Understanding (MoU)
5. Between sessions: email to mediator (not directly between parties)

**The gap:** No shared digital workspace between sessions. The mediator is the manual hub.

## How consent orders get drafted

1. Solicitor drafts in Word
2. Sent to other party for review (track changes)
3. 2-6 rounds of amendments, 3 weeks per round
4. D81 prepared alongside (23 pages since March 2022)
5. Submitted via MyHMCTS portal as PDF
6. Judge reviews — approve, reject, or request amendments
7. Processing: 6-10 weeks

**Track changes in Word is the state of the art.**

## What judges complain about

- **Mr Justice Mostyn:** Costs are "grotesque" and "a scandal" — the system "offends the key constitutional principle that justice should never have to be purchased"
- **Incomplete disclosure** at FDR hearings — missing valuations, outstanding CETVs
- **Poorly prepared bundles** — not paginated, not indexed, duplicates
- **D81 Section 10** left blank — most common reason consent orders are returned

## Competitive landscape

### Consumer-facing

| Platform | Model | Strengths | Gaps |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Amicable** | Fixed-price service (£750-2,500) | Both parties on same platform; D81/consent order prep | Joint-couple only; no AI; service not tool |
| **SeparateSpace** | Subscription guidance (£25-39/mo) | 6-step pathway; guides and workshops | Guidance only; no operational tool |
| **Splitifi** | AI "Divorce Operating System" | Financial workflow; attorney dashboard; role-based sharing | US-origin; UK newer; primarily US jurisdiction |

### B2B / Professional

| Platform | Model | Strengths | Gaps |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Quantum Cloud** | Form E drafting for solicitors | Cross-party collaboration; clients input data; 100+ forms | No AI; manual data entry; no intelligence |
| **Armalytix** | FCA-regulated Open Banking | Transaction analysis; income/outgoings; suspicious flagging | No collaboration; no Form E; B2B only |
| **adieu.ai** | AI paralegal (Australian) | Autonomous document sourcing from clients; 3.5 sec vs 6 hrs | B2B; AU origin; UK unclear |
| **LEAP** | Practice management | Financial Statement app; client portal | Generic; no AI; no cross-party workspace |
| **Clio** | Case management | Client portal; co-counsel sharing | Generic legal tool; no family law workflow |

### Bundle preparation (downstream)

BundleCreator, Zylpha, XBundle — exist solely because court bundle preparation is so painful.

## The strategic gap

**Nobody occupies the intersection of:**
- Consumer-first (not B2B)
- AI-powered extraction (not manual form-filling)
- Collaboration-enabled (both parties see the same data)
- Full lifecycle (disclosure through to consent order)

Quantum Cloud has collaboration but no AI. Armalytix has AI but no collaboration. Splitifi has both but is US-centric. Amicable is a service, not a tool.

## Regulatory context

- **SRA:** Principles-based, not prescriptive. No approval scheme for tools. Solicitors must verify any tool's output meets professional standards. Key requirement: **auditability and traceability** to source documents.
- **Resolution Together:** "One lawyer, two clients" model (580+ practitioners). Both parties copied on all correspondence. Transparency is the innovation. Aligns with shared workspace concept.
- **Express Financial Remedy Pilot:** Front-loads all disclosure before first hearing. Creates urgency for faster, more efficient disclosure tools.
- **Law Commission (Dec 2024):** Current law "does not provide a cohesive framework." Reform consultation expected Spring 2026. If reform moves toward formulaic approaches, structured financial data becomes even more valuable.

## International precedents

- **Australia:** Digital filing via Commonwealth Courts Portal. adieu.ai provides AI paralegal services — 3.5 seconds vs 6 hours for disclosure schedules.
- **US:** Splitifi — most technically advanced consumer platform. Role-based sharing, time-limited access links, patented technology.
- **Canada:** PartUs — attorney-facing document collection from clients.

**Pattern:** Digital filing portals exist (AU), AI assistance exists (adieu.ai), consumer platforms exist (Splitifi). But none combines consumer-first intelligence with cross-party collaboration.
