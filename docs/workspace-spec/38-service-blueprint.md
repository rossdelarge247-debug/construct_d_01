# Spec 38 — Service Blueprint: As-Is Process vs Decouple

**Date:** 17 April 2026
**Purpose:** Map the complete divorce financial disclosure and settlement process. At each step: what happens today, the pain points, the cost, and what Decouple does differently. Goal: identify where the product eliminates the need for professional intermediaries entirely.

## The headline

The current process takes **12-18 months** and costs **£14,561 per person** on average. Of the ~40 distinct steps, approximately **80% are administrative** (gathering, compiling, formatting, communicating) and **20% require genuine legal judgment** (is this fair? is this enforceable?). Decouple automates the 80%.

## Where solicitors/mediators are still needed (the genuine 20%)

| Task | Why human judgment needed | Decouple's role |
|---|---|---|
| **Complex asset valuation** | Business valuations, pension actuary reports, overseas assets | Product flags when professional valuation is needed |
| **Tax implications** | CGT on property transfer, pension tax charges | Product can surface the question, not answer it |
| **Coercive control situations** | Safety, power dynamics, cannot self-serve | Product detects indicators, routes to professional |
| **Contested cases going to court** | Legal representation required at hearings | Product provides the disclosure data, solicitor represents |
| **Consent order review** | Legal sign-off that the order is enforceable | Product generates draft, solicitor reviews (1-2 hours, not 10-20) |
| **Unusual structures** | Trusts, offshore assets, complex business shareholdings | Product captures what it can, flags complexity |

**The pattern:** Decouple handles the standard case completely. For complexity, it provides the data and routes to professionals. The professional's role shifts from "full-service administrator" to "specialist reviewer" — surgeon, not GP.

## The disruption thesis

**For the 60% of cases that are straightforward (employed, homeowner/renter, standard assets, willing to cooperate):** Decouple eliminates the need for a solicitor entirely except for a ~£500 consent order review. Total cost drops from ~£14,500 per person to ~£1,100 (£593 court fee + optional professional review).

**For the next 25% (moderate complexity or mild conflict):** Decouple handles disclosure and negotiation, with on-demand professional review for specific items. Total cost drops to ~£2,000-4,000 per person (from ~£14,500).

**For the 15% (high conflict, complex assets, non-engagement):** Decouple provides the best possible disclosure foundation. The timeline and evidence trail are useful to their solicitor. But they'll still need full representation.

The product's total addressable disruption: **85% of cases can be handled with minimal or no professional involvement.** That's the ambition.
