## What each path is good at

### Solicitor-led is good for
- High-conflict cases where formal legal representation is needed
- Complex estates (trusts, overseas assets, business valuations)
- Situations with significant power imbalance
- Cases already heading to court
- Parties who want someone else to handle everything

### Mediator-led is good for
- Parties who want to negotiate together but need help facilitating
- Situations where emotional conversations need a neutral third party
- Couples where one is more dominant — mediator manages the dynamic
- First-time disclosure with someone explaining the process
- Producing a structured conversation, not just documents

### Decouple is good for
- The 60% who are already aligned or just need structure
- Tech-comfortable users who want to self-serve
- Couples who want transparency and evidence (trust through data)
- Parties who want speed and cost control
- Anyone who finds solicitor/mediator offices intimidating

### Mediator + Decouple (the best of both) is good for
- Most mediation cases — the product eliminates admin, mediator handles the conversations
- Mediators' practices (run more cases per month, better outcomes)
- Parties who need human facilitation on hard items but can self-serve on the rest

## The strategic positioning

```
                High conflict / high complexity
                              │
                              │
                   Solicitor-led
                              │
                              │
Low human                     │                     High human
support need ─────────────────┼───────────────── support need
                              │
                              │
                   Decouple only ◄──── Mediator + Decouple
                              │
                              │
                Low conflict / low complexity
```

Decouple's sweet spot: **low conflict + low-to-moderate complexity**. This is 60% of the market (Amicable's "already aligned" and "need a nudge" segments).

Mediator + Decouple extends reach into **moderate conflict** cases (another 20-25%).

Solicitor-led remains necessary for **high conflict / high complexity** (15-20%).

## What this means for the business

### User segments

1. **Decouple only** — 60% of the market. Direct consumer subscription. £200-300 per couple.
2. **Mediator partnerships** — 25% of the market. B2B2C partnerships with FMC-registered mediators. Mediators white-label the product; mediators charge their usual hourly rate; Decouple gets a per-case fee.
3. **Solicitor referrals** — 15% of the market. Even high-conflict cases benefit from structured disclosure. Solicitors refer clients to use Decouple for disclosure; they handle representation.

### The disruption arc

**Year 1:** Serve the 60% directly. Prove the model. Build trust.

**Year 2:** Partner with mediators. They realise Decouple makes their practice more efficient AND expands what they can handle.

**Year 3:** Become the default disclosure platform. Solicitors and mediators routinely expect clients to arrive with a Decouple-generated financial picture.

The end-state: **Decouple is to divorce what Xero is to accounting** — the infrastructure that everyone uses, with professional services layered on top for complexity.
