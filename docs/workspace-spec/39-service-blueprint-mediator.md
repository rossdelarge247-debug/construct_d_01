# Spec 39 — Service Blueprint: Mediator-Led Process

**Date:** 17 April 2026
**Purpose:** Map the mediator-led divorce financial process end-to-end. For each step: as-is, pain points, and what Decouple does differently.

## The headline

Mediator-led process takes **2-4 months** and costs **£600-1,500 per person** on average. Faster and cheaper than solicitor-led, but **still requires a solicitor at the end** to draft the consent order from the Memorandum of Understanding. The mediator cannot give legal advice or draft legally binding orders.

### Step 2.2: Gather financial information (individually)
| | As-is | Pain | Decouple |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Action** | Each party completes the mediator's disclosure spreadsheet (a simpler Form E — less onerous but still comprehensive). | **Time** | 2-4 weeks per party | — | 15 minutes (+ CETV wait) |

### Step 2.3: Disclosure exchange
| | As-is | Pain | Decouple |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Action** | Both parties send completed spreadsheets to mediator. Mediator compiles into a combined view ("schedule of assets"). | Spreadsheet-based, static. Mediator manually consolidates. If one party is late, everyone waits. | Progressive disclosure — Party A shares document, Party B joins, document merges live. No exchange event. |
| **Cost** | Included in mediator hourly rate | — | Free |
| **Time** | 1 week (often delayed by late disclosure) | — | Immediate |

### Step 2.4: No evidence verification
| | As-is | Pain | Decouple |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Action** | Mediator asks parties to bring documents but has no verification mechanism. "My ex brought a list with totals and the mediator said nothing." | **The single biggest weakness of mediation.** No bank evidence check. No credit reference. | **Cost** | — | A user quote: "Mediators are not prepared to offer any view on the reasonableness of either claim" | Free |
| **Opportunity** | **Decouple fixes mediation's biggest flaw.** Mediation's value is the collaborative process; its weakness is unverified data. Decouple adds the verification layer. |

| **Cost** | £500-2,000 per party | — | £0-500 (review only) |
| **Time** | 2-6 weeks | — | Days |

### Step 5.2-5.4: Court submission and approval
Same as solicitor-led: submit consent order + D81 + court fee (£593). Wait 6-10 weeks for judicial review. Order sealed or queried.

## What Decouple adds to mediation

1. **Evidence verification** (the single biggest weakness of mediation) — bank connection, credit check, structured document handling
2. **Async between sessions** — proposals, discussions, evidence exchange happen between mediator meetings, making sessions shorter and more focused
3. **Mediator as guest** — mediator joins the shared document, sees everything, facilitates the hard parts
4. **Automated MoU + consent order generation** — eliminates the drafting costs
5. **Structured agreement data** — flows directly into legal documents
6. **Progress visibility** — both parties and the mediator can see convergence happening

The mediator's role transforms from "administrative facilitator + data keeper + document drafter" to **"expert in the hard conversations."** Higher-value work, less billable time — but also less total time needed.

**For mediators who adopt Decouple:** same hourly rate, fewer hours per case, better outcomes. They can take more cases per month and deliver higher-quality outcomes.

**For users:** mediation becomes significantly cheaper (~£1,100-2,500 per person instead of £1,500-4,000) and dramatically faster (6-10 weeks instead of 12-20 weeks).
